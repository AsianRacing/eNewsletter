Dear all,
 
Welcome to the newsletter produced by the ARF Council on Anti-Illegal Betting & Related Financial Crime (ARF Council). The newsletter is bi-weekly, to provide a more timely overview of the situation relating to illegal betting and related financial crime.
 
The lead stories in this edition are once again the criminal case against the Macao casino junket, Suncity as well as the inquiries into casino operations in Australia.
 
In Macau, the former Chairman of casino junket Suncity and his associates have been charged with offences relating to HKD 823.7 billion (USD 104 billion) in illegal betting volume, as well as almost HKD3.6 billion (USD 460 million) in illicit phone and online betting that was reportedly ‘laundered’ via transfers from front companies and underground banks in mainland China that were then ‘mixed’ into Suncity revenue accounts.
 
In Brisbane, Australia, a casino operator has been found to have allowed the use of Union Pay credit cards for AUD 900 million (USD 623 million) of transactions for gambling chip purchases disguised as invoices.
 
Both of these cases illustrate the huge financial scale and cross border complexity of online betting (on racing and other sports), online gambling (on casino games), and related money laundering that is conducted by transnational organised crime groups.
 
I hope that this newsletter gives you some insight into the continued global problem of illegal betting and its negative impact.

Best wishes,
Martin Purbrick
Chairperson, Asian Racing Federation Council on Anti-illegal Betting & Related Financial Crime

Australia


Star inquiry wraps up amid accusations of further lawbreaking
Australian Financial Review, 27 June 2022

Star Entertainment may have broken the NSW’ Unlawful Gambling Act by allowing high-roller junket operator Suncity to run its own casino within Star’s harbourside property, the inquiry into the gambling giant heard on Monday.

On the final day of hearings, Ms Sharp [counsel assisting the review] also cast doubt on Star’s argument that its long-running China Union Pay debit card scheme did not pose heightened money laundering risks.

Under the UnionPay program, Star facilitated $900 million of banned gambling transactions which disguised gambling chip purchases as fake hotel stays by way of sham “hotel invoices”.

China


Criminal group headed by Alvin Chau said to have caused HK$8.2 bln in lost gaming tax revenue to SAR
Macau Business, 23 June 2022

The criminal group allegedly headed by Alvin Chau Cheok Wa and other former middle and senior management executives of the now-shuttered Suncity junket operator caused HK$8.2 billion (US$1 billion) in lost gaming tax revenue to the Macau SAR, the official indictment accessed by Macau News Agency alleges.

The first court session of the trial involving Alvin Chau and 20 other defendants has been scheduled for September 2.

The indictment documents paint a picture of an illegal gambling organization managed in detail by Chau and engaged in complex illicit side-betting operations in VIP rooms since at least 2013 in properties managed by all six gaming concessionaires and subconcessionaires in Macau.

The same group organised illegal online and proxy betting operations that allowed Suncity VIP Room account holders to place bets in Vietnam, Cambodia and the Philippines, the prosecution alleges.

Mr Chau stands accused of leading a syndicate that managed more than HK$823.7 billion in illegal betting volume, with about HK$21.5 billion of profit.

Almost HK$3.6 billion in illicit phone and online betting were also said to have been ‘laundered’ via transfers from front companies and underground banks in mainland China that were then ‘mixed’ into Suncity revenue accounts.

About HK$616.9 million and RMB17 million from side-betting proceeds were also allegedly laundered by falsely using asset management, investment projects, commercial financing and other normal forms of trade to conceal the true nature and origin.

India


Indian MP Jailed For Two Years After Illegal Gambling Bust
Female Cricket, 22 June 2022

A senior MP in India has been sentenced to two years of rigorous imprisonment by a court in Gujarat’s Panchmahals district. He was one of 25 people arrested while partaking in illegal gambling activities at a Shivrajpur Village resort in the Panchmahals district.

The entire incident was a living embodiment of what the pro-legalization lobby for gambling in India argues against. Instead of legalizing gambling in India and imposing taxation on regulated gambling houses (which in turn brings a boost to employment opportunities), the government continues to waste police resources on expensive and time-consuming sting operations.

Sweden


New Measures by Swedish Government to Ensure a Safe Gambling Market
Gambling News, 15 June 2022

The Swedish government has submitted a report to Sweden’s Law Council (Lagrådet) with proposals, whose objective is to exclude illegal players from the Swedish gambling market and counteract the manipulation of results in sports or the so-called match-fixing. The new measures should be introduced in Swedish law before 1st July 2023.

Increased opportunities are being put forward so that licensees and sports federations can process personal data in order to uncover match-fixing. It is also suggested that licensees should be obliged to provide, at the request of the Police, all necessary information when the investigation is underway into crimes in connection with gambling games.

Feds Close Alleged $34M Cleveland-Area Gambling Operation
Money Laundering, Tax Evasion Among The Alleged Crimes
Card Player, 24 June 2022

A federal grand jury in Cleveland, Ohio, has charged a local man with running a more than $30 million illegal gambling operation. [He] was charged with tax evasion, filing false tax returns, witness tampering, falsifying records and five separate conspiracies to operate illegal gambling businesses, defraud the IRS and commit money laundering.

The Asian Racing Federation Council on Anti-illegal Betting and Related Financial Crime

The Asian Racing Federation Council on Anti-illegal Betting and Related Financial Crime was established in 2017 as a think tank aimed at combatting illegal betting and related financial crime. The ARF Council now comprises 18 members from organisations engaged in horse racing and sports integrity, law enforcement, the United Nations, and academia.

The ARF Council’s purpose is to research and share the scale and negative impacts of illegal betting, particularly as it relates to horse racing and sports integrity, and to foster international collaboration among stakeholders, such as horse racing operators and authorities, gambling regulators, law enforcement agencies, and government policy makers to raise awareness of the threat and to combat the negative impact of illegal betting and other financial crimes to horse racing, other sports, and to society.
