Dear all,
 
Welcome to the newsletter produced by the ARF Council on Anti-Illegal Betting & Related Financial Crime. The newsletter is bi-weekly and provides a timely overview of the situation relating to illegal betting and related financial crime.
 
The first two reports in this newsletter highlight the links between sex-trafficking, prostitution and online betting in Southeast Asia. In the Philippines, a report on this pertaining to betting companies registered in the Philippines was presented by senators to the Filipino Senate. These powerful articles underline how the online gambling industry in Asia is linked to money laundering, drug dealing, and sex trafficking – this is the financial crime related to illegal betting and gambling.
 
Coinciding with the ongoing Beijing Winter Olympics, the International Olympic Committee (IOC) is reported by Sports Insider to have been building awareness of how “match-fixing and illegal betting completely ruin the passion of sport that all athletes have”. There is significant monitoring of betting markets by commercial service providers in partnership with the IOC, which needs to cover illegal betting markets which are often less visible than the legal markets but where betting related to competition manipulation is more likely to take place. The action illustrates the major threat of illegal betting to sports integrity.
 
In this edition we have a special section on the continued crackdown on casino junkets from Macau by the local and also Mainland China authorities. On 30 January, the Macau Judiciary Police arrested the CEO of both Macau Legend (a large casino resort operator) and of the Tak Chun Group (one of the largest junkets) in connection with allegedly engaging in illegal online gambling, running a triad society crime syndicate, and money laundering. The police stated that the case was related to the earlier arrests of the CEO of Suncity, the largest junket operator in Macau. Both are alleged to have involved huge online gambling and sports betting with operations in the Philippines. It is now increasingly apparent that certain Macau casino junkets are considered by authorities to have played a major part in the growth of online illegal betting and that these groups involve many people with a triad society criminal background. We expect that this continued crackdown will set the tone for the future, with illegal online betting recognised as an organised crime activity that brings these groups huge profits and must be combatted.
 
I hope that this newsletter gives you some insight into the continued global problem of illegal betting and its negative impact.

Best wishes,
Martin Purbrick
Chairperson, Asian Racing Federation Council on Anti-illegal Betting & Related Financial Crime

Gambling with lives 
Josimar, 31 January 2022

A report on the links between sex-trafficking, prostitution and gambling companies registered in the Philippines was presented last week to the Filipino Senate. Its conclusions are shocking. Tens of thousands of young women, sometimes underage girls, are employed and abused in a sex industry aimed almost solely at the huge number of Chinese workers who have flocked, most of them illegally, to Manila to work for offshore bookmakers, of which at least eight are current commercial partners of Premier League football clubs…

The astonishing growth of the e-gambling industry in the archipelago over the last fifteen years was associated with sex trafficking, as it is with money-laundering, drugs dealing and other criminal activities, many if not most of which involve Chinese gangs – triads – whose presence and influence is felt especially strongly in the gambling enclave of Macau. 
 
Online fraud: how Chinese nationals forced to run internet scams in Cambodia earn millions for their captors, sometimes paying with their lives
South China Morning Post, 30 January 2022

Thousands of Chinese nationals, some as young as 15, are estimated to have fallen prey to transnational crime groups trafficking people from China to Cambodia … Many toil in slave-like conditions, serving online gambling and scamming companies.
The scamming operations are an outgrowth of an online gambling industry that has exploded in [Sihanoukville] in recent years.

The Financial Action Task Force (FATF), a France-based intergovernmental watchdog, placed Cambodia on its money-laundering watch list in 2019. When, that same year, Cambodian authorities banned online gambling, it unleashed a capital and labour flight. Many Sihanoukville casinos closed and at least 200,000 Chinese nationals reportedly left the country.

Thereafter, some casinos and hotels appear to have become fronts for cyber-slavery operations and have been involved in online scams. The schemes described by those who perpetuated them include investments in dubious stocks, cryptocurrency, lotteries and gambling scams.

Rachakonda cops bust illegal horse race betting racket, arrest five
Telegana Today, 4 February 2022

Hyderabad: The Rachakonda Police Special Operations Team (SOT) along with the Meerpet police busted an illegal horse race betting racket and arrested five persons…Police seized Rs 42 lakh (USD 56,000)….
While organising betting on horse races in Hyderabad, Bengaluru, Mysuru, Mumbai, Kolkata and Chennai by watching updates on apps, Joji Reddy used to post screenshots of the race in the WhatsApp groups. The winning amount was transferred to them through e-wallets only.

“During the nationwide lockdown due to the pandemic, people remained indoors and there were no horse races in the country. The races resumed only in November 2020, but many people preferred to bet on horses through mobile phones using various betting apps,” said Mahesh Bhagwat, Police Commissioner, Rachakonda.

“The punters were aware that the process was illegal. Joji Reddy accepted online bets and money through e-wallets Google Pay, PhonePe, to record the money transactions (in and out),” Bhagwat said.

European Parliament's agreement on new digital rules involving illegal iGaming praised by European Lotteries
Yogonet, 21 January 2022

MEPs agreed on a draft set of measures to tackle illegal content, to ensure platforms are held accountable for their algorithms, and improve content moderation.

The Digital Services Act (SA) proposal defines responsibilities and accountability for providers of intermediary services, and in particular online platforms. It also establishes a “notice and action” mechanism, as well as safeguards, for the removal of illegal products, services or content online.

The European Lotteries, the European umbrella organization of national lotteries operating games of chance, welcomed the new provisions, which are also set to fight illegal online gambling.

Super Bowl LVI expected to generate $1B in legal bets, doubling last year's handle
Yogonet, 3 February 2022

Super Bowl LVI could lead to the US generating about $1 billion in legal bets, according to projections by PlayUSA released on Wednesday. The network, which provides news and analysis of the US gaming industry, believes that if sportsbooks reach those estimates, it would roughly double last year’s estimated Super Bowl handle of more than $500 million.

More than 166.9 million people will be able to place legal bets during the next Super Bowl edition, across 30 states and Washington DC, which have all legalized the market in some form. The number is significantly up from the 120 million people allowed to place legal bets in 21 jurisdictions for last year’s edition.

The Asian Racing Federation Council on Anti-illegal Betting and Related Financial Crime

The Asian Racing Federation Council on Anti-illegal Betting and Related Financial Crime was established in 2017 as a think tank aimed at combatting illegal betting and related financial crime. The ARF Council now comprises 15 members from organisations engaged in horse racing and sports integrity, law enforcement, the United Nations, and academia.

The ARF Council’s purpose is to research and share the scale and negative impacts of illegal betting, particularly as it relates to horse racing and sports integrity, and to foster international collaboration among stakeholders, such as horse racing operators and authorities, gambling regulators, law enforcement agencies, and government policy makers to raise awareness of the threat and to combat the negative impact of illegal betting and other financial crimes to horse racing, other sports, and to society.
